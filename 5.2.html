Different Working Patterns

Working Pattern Definitions
Full time A contract where someone workers 8 hours per day, for five days
Part-time A contract where someone works fewer hours per week than a full-time employee, in shifts
Flexi-time A contract where workers can choose their working time, but will have to complete the day's work
Job sharing A contract where two or more part-time workers divide their job, replacing one full-time worker
Compressed hours A contract where employees can work longer hours per day but over fewer days, completing the workload sooner

Reasons for different working patterns

  1. To suit employees' lifestyles or home circumstances
  2. To allow the employees to avoid rush-hour commuting in the morning and evening
  3. To make the employees more highly motivated

Advantages of different working patterns

  • Contented employees - reduction in recruitment and training costs for new staff
  • Flexi-time allows the shops to remain open for longer hours
  • Job sharing ensures the company has more than one person with a particular skill
  • Compressed hours lead staff to be more focused on their work
  • Give more flexibility during busy times and during staff sickness
